Charles E. Burchfield

1893-1967




Story


Charles E. Burchfield (Charles Ephraim Burchfield) born April 9, 1893 Ashtbula (Ohio, United States) and died January 19, 1967 in West Seneca, New York, is an American painter attached to the school of American realism and particularly recognized for his watercolours.

In the 1940s, Burchfield, probing the mysteries of nature in an attempt to reveal his inner emotions, said: "An artist should paint not what he sees in nature, but what is there, to that, he has to invent symbols., makes his work seem even more real than what he has in front of him." He followed this artistic vision until the end of his life, creating some of his works the most mystical. The Burchfield Penney Art Center (or Burchfield Penney) is an arts and educational institution that is part of Buffalo State College (New York), dedicated to the painter Charles E. Burchfield. Founded in 1966 as the Charles E. Burchfield Center, the center . has a museum, library and activity space for the arts.
